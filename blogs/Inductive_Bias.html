Summary of Inductive Bias (naive)

Inductive bias is the assumption an ML model makes to generalize across unseen data. For example, linear regression assumes a linear relationship, while K-nearest neighbors assumes neighboring points share the same class. This differs from data bias in the sense that inductive bias is a necessary and powerful tool, while data bias is an unwanted side effect of training.
