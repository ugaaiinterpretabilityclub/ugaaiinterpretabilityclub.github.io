Local Hidden Variables, Trichotomy, and Double Negation

The proof by contradiction of Local Hidden Variable theory uses the law of trichotomy to reach not-not LHVs. The law of trichotomy does not hold in the BHK framework. Furthermore, not-not P does not necessarily equal P. Thus the specific proof fails in our BHK framework.

Undecidability of Reals and Measurement in Physical Reality

Our reals are undecidable. We can prove this by constructing a piecewise cauchy sequence that halts given a flip in the truth value of an unresolved proposition P(n) for n in the naturals. And of course, our numbers are the equivelance classes of cauchy sequences, so our numbers are undecidable. This coincides with physical measurement: we resolve truth up to a certain resolution, but past the point of precision our measurement permits, the truth value is undecidable.
